Luke 1:1-25 (The Announcement of John the Baptist's Birth)

1. Luke begins his Gospel by stating his purpose (1:1-4). What does he say he is doing and why? What does this tell us about the reliability of Luke’s Gospel?

2. Zechariah and Elizabeth are described as “righteous before God, living blamelessly according to all the commandments and statutes of the Lord” (1:6). Yet, they were childless. How does this challenge common assumptions about blessings and obedience?

Luke 1:26-56 (The Annunciation and Mary's Visit to Elizabeth)

3. Compare and contrast the reactions of Zechariah (1:18-20) and Mary (1:34-38) to the angel’s announcements. What are the key differences?

4. Mary's response, "Let it be to me according to your word" (1:38), is a powerful statement of surrender. In what areas of your life are you struggling to surrender to God's will? How can Mary's example encourage you?

Luke 2:1-20 (The Birth of Jesus)

5. Why do you think it was important for Jesus to be born in Bethlehem, and in such humble circumstances (a manger)?

Luke 4:1-13 (The Temptation of Jesus)

6. Jesus was tempted in the wilderness. While we may not face the same specific temptations, we all face temptations. What can we learn from Jesus' response to temptation (using Scripture) that can help us in our own struggles?
