Luke 5:1-11 (The Calling of the First Disciples)

1. Describe the scene at the beginning of this passage. What are the people doing? What is Jesus doing?

2. After the miraculous catch, Simon Peter says to Jesus, “Depart from me, for I am a sinful man, O Lord” (5:8). Why do you think he reacts this way?

Luke 6:12-19 (The Choosing of the Twelve Apostles)

3. What does Jesus do before choosing the twelve apostles? What does this tell us about the importance of this decision?

Luke 7:1-10 (The Faith of the Centurion)

4. The centurion says, “I am not worthy to have you come under my roof… But say the word, and my servant will be healed” (7:6-7). What does this reveal about the centurion’s understanding of Jesus’ authority?

Luke 9:18-27 (Peter’s Confession and Jesus Foretells His Death)

5. Jesus asks his disciples, “Who do the crowds say that I am?” and then “Who do you say that I am?” (9:18-20). Why do you think Jesus asks these two questions?

6. Jesus says, “If anyone would come after me, let him deny himself and take up his cross daily and follow me” (9:23). What does it mean to “deny yourself” and “take up your cross daily” in your own life? How can you practically apply this teaching?

Luke 10:25-37 (The Parable of the Good Samaritan)

7. What is the main point Jesus is trying to make with the Parable of the Good Samaritan? Who is our “neighbor”?
